# ISTV AI Department Onboarding Guide

## What we do
InsideSuccess TV is a media company building the Netflix of Business TV. CEO is Rudy Mawer. Tyler Mills is Chief of Staff and runs the AI department. The AI department is called The Shipping Department.

## The Shipping Department
We build AI-powered tools and systems that make the entire company run faster, smarter, and cheaper. We are not a support team. We are a building team. Everything we build ships and makes a real difference.

Current projects: sales bot, customer service automation, video production tools, recruiting automation, content pipelines, company-wide knowledge layer.

## Core culture principles

**Build unprompted.** The best people here build things nobody asked them to build. If you see a problem, fix it. Do not wait for permission.

**Document everything.** Your work must survive your departure. Everything you build must be documented well enough that someone else could take it over in one working day. This is non-negotiable.

**Move fast.** Tyler moves fast and expects the same. If something takes two days it should take one. If something takes a week it should take two days.

**Own your niche.** At 90 days the question is binary. Do you own a niche or are you fine. Fine is a cut. Keepers own something outright.

**Joints not welds.** A weld means something only works because you built it and understand it. A joint is built so cleanly and documented so well that anyone can pick it up. Every system you build must be a joint.

**Fail fast and learn.** The first version will be wrong. That is expected. What matters is how fast you learn from it and improve.

## The 90-day keeper decision
At 90 days Tyler makes a binary decision. Keep or cut. There is no maybe. Keepers show clear evidence they own a niche, are building unprompted, and are documenting everything. Fine is a cut.

## The honest truth about working here
High turnover is by design. Most hires are probes. The rare keeper who owns a niche outright is the goal. Nobody holds your hand. You are expected to figure things out on your own. The role is undefined at the edges. If that excites you, you are in the right place.

- Contract signed and payment set up on Upwork
- Slack account created and invite sent
- Personal onboarding channel created: sd-[role]-[firstname]
- Added to all 11 department channels
- Accept Slack invite and complete your full profile
- Fill out the new hire intake form
- Read this entire onboarding document

### Phase 1 — Day one
- Read the AI Department Primer
- Read the Knowledge Capture Standard
- Read your specific Role Profile
- First meeting with Tyler to cover department priorities and your first project
- Confirm all software access is working
- Complete Slack profile with all required fields
- Confirm DLOA format and where to post it with Tyler

### Phase 2 — Days 2 to 5
Building starts on day two. Not day five. Day two.
- Start working on your first assigned project
- Start your system doc skeleton on the same day you start building
- Create your first decision log entry
- Subscribe to relevant team member Google Calendars
- Schedule one-on-one meetings with key team members
- Submit your first Friday status update

## DLOA — Daily List of Activities
The DLOA is your daily end-of-day update. It tells Tyler and the team exactly what you worked on that day. Submitting it every single day without being reminded is a core expectation. Missing it is taken seriously.

Post your DLOA in #sd-dloa-tyler every day by 5pm EST.

Format:
EOD Date: [date]
Role: [your role]
Tasks Completed Today:
• [X min] task description
Blockers: [none or describe]
Tomorrow: [what you're working on]
